Personal Statement

Art has always been my passion, compulsion, and career. Through childhood, adolescence, marriage, having children, college, work, more work, Dublin, Washington, California, Texas, Oregon, Missouri, Paris, Rome, Papua New Guinea, and back to Dublin, I have always created art. My style and medium changed often. I see this long period not as time lost but as my path to explore my artistic voice by having no critics or expectations preventing me from experimenting.
I came to realize that I created as a means to understand how I experience and interact in the world through my emotions. Whether I was creating with paint, clay, glass, wax, plastic, wood, steel or any number of found objects, I was always expressing these emotions. The process that I use to create is to first map out the emotions from a significant event in my life. I explore each feeling and refine and group them and show their interactions. I end up with a diagram that represents the confusing and conflicting feelings about the event. (Interestingly, negative events are much more complex than happy events.) From this I create an individual sculpture or painting representing each individual emotion. Sometimes a viewer will immediately understand the emotion that I am representing, but often a completely different emotion will be experienced. These are the conversations that are very exciting to me. This is where I, as an artist, connect with a person on an emotional level. We share this world but we experience it differently. These differences are the beauty in each individual.

- Bredan McCabe, Artist.
About
